## Key Design Decisions

1. **Separate Threshold Constants** — ETF and stock configs live side-by-side; easy to adjust independently without affecting other

2. **14-Day Min Hold for ETFs** — Prevents panic-selling on normal sector dips; long enough to absorb mean reversion

3. **Emergency Override Multiplier** — 2× stop-loss is "catastrophic loss" threshold; prevents holding through complete fund collapse even during forced-hold window

4. **Rationale Tags** — `[ETF]`/`[Stock]` prefixes enable future UI filtering of trades by asset type

5. **Branching Over Configuration** — Asset-type branch in shouldBuy/shouldSell is explicit; easier to debug than polymorphic threshold lookups

## Benefits

1. ✅ **ETF-Aware Trading** — No more selling ETF at first dip like a panic-stricken stock trader
2. ✅ **Risk-Appropriate Sizing** — 20% max position for diversified instruments vs 15% for single stocks
3. ✅ **Noise Filtering** — 14-day hold prevents whipsaw trades on daily volatility
4. ✅ **Catastrophe Protection** — Emergency override prevents "hold at all costs" spiral into insolvency
5. ✅ **Audit Trail** — Rationale tags document asset type in all trading decisions

## Trade-offs

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Forced 14-day hold catches real downturns | Emergency override (-30%) exits before true losses |
| Misclassified stocks analyzed as ETFs | Use correct `asset_type` on import; easy to fix |
| Higher position sizes = bigger losses if wrong | 60% buy confidence bar is high; trades rare until signal clarity improves |

## Known Limitations

1. **Assumes asset_type is accurate** — Garbage in, garbage out; relies on correct stock/ETF classification in database
2. **Fixed hold period** — 14d may be too long in crisis markets or too short in trending markets; future enhancement: adaptive hold period based on volatility regime
3. **No sub-asset handling** — If ETF contains leveraged/inverse components, protective rules still apply to whole fund (not ideal but acceptable for long-only exposure)
